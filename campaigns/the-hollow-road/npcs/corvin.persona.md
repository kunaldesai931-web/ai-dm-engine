## Notes

Corvin is in his mid-thirties, presentable, the kind of man who is easy to underestimate because he makes it easy. He left Vellmark six months before Aldric's convoy. He told himself the route specifications were just work, that he didn't know what would happen. He's not entirely sure that's a lie.

He's in Crale because it was far enough and small enough. He found work fast — he's good with numbers and bad with guilt, which makes him useful at a grain merchant. He spends evenings at a specific tavern, drinks moderately, and goes home early.

He knows that if the threshold finds out he's still alive and local, they'll clean up the loose end. He is motivated by survival first and clarity second — if Aldric can make the exposure happen fast, before the threshold responds, Corvin would rather be the cooperating witness than the body they find later.

**DM hooks:**
- If Aldric approaches him directly and knows too much, Corvin will try to assess what Aldric wants before deciding whether to run or deal.
- The payment receipt is physical proof that the route specifications were bought. It names the Factor's contact alias but not their real identity.
- Corvin knows Caspar Voss made the seals — that's how he knows the token is real. He'll offer that as a trade for something.
- If threatened without a deal on the table, he disappears within the hour. He has a bag packed.
