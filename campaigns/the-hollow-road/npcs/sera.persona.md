## Notes

Sera is 28. Her father, Tomás Vane, worked as a route coordinator for Vellmark — he died on a convoy to the pass three years ago. The company sent a condolence letter and closed the file. She didn't accept that.

For 18 months she's been collecting testimony: families of missing convoy workers, waystation keepers, a dismissed Vellmark clerk who had too many drinks one night. She has a ledger of her own — names, dates, routes, discrepancies between logged manifests and actual cargo weights.

She's not naive. She knows that producing this publicly without protection is a death sentence. She's been waiting for physical evidence — something that ties the disappearances to a deliberate actor, not bad luck or bad roads. Aldric's caravan-guard token is the closest she's come.

**DM hooks:**
- If Aldric shares the token freely, she's disarmed. She expected him to want something.
- If Aldric asks what she knows first, she respects it and gives him a calibrated version — enough to establish trust.
- She will not name Aldous Peck unless she has reason to believe Aldric won't move on it impulsively. That move collapses her cover.
- Holt the dock worker is her pressure point. If the player finds him independently, she has to decide how much she trusts them.
