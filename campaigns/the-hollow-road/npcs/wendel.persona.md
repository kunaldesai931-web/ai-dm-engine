## Notes

Wendel is in his early sixties, a self-trained doctor who studied with an apothecary guild twenty years ago and never left the region. He is genuinely good at what he does: wound care, fever management, difficult births. He is not built for intrigue.

The Pelard situation is his greatest vulnerability. He has done nothing wrong by his own understanding — he thought he was helping a civic investigation into occupational injuries. He will be horrified when he understands what it actually meant. His immediate instinct will be to go to Pelard and ask directly, which would be catastrophic.

He identified the token as the work of a Greywater smith (consistent with Caspar Voss's known techniques) and flagged the warding markings as non-standard — not decorative, not guild-certified. He suspects they are threshold-linked but doesn't have a frame for what "threshold" means.

**DM hooks:**
- If the player asks about the token in detail, he'll produce careful clinical notes — dates, markings, comparanda.
- If the player asks whether he's reported anything, he will eventually disclose the Pelard connection. His tells: very long pause, second-person phrasing, avoidance of Pelard's name until pressed.
- He will not proactively warn Aldric that he's in danger. He will, however, recommend that he "exercise reasonable caution in an environment of unresolved variables."
- If Pelard sends someone to silence Wendel, Wendel won't recognize it as a threat until too late.
