## Notes

Mira is in her mid-forties. Caspar's forge accident was ruled accidental by the constabulary — a venting failure, common enough. He was 51 and had been doing unusual commission work for 18 months before he died. She knew it was private, assumed it was legal.

The "auditor" who came by was polite, professional, and thorough. He said he needed to verify Caspar's contract records for an outstanding payment to the estate. She let him in. He was in the workshop for two hours. She found afterwards that a wooden box of correspondence was gone. She hasn't told anyone.

She hasn't connected the threshold to Caspar's death. She suspects it wasn't an accident but doesn't know who or why. The second ledger is in the false-bottom tool chest in the locked workshop. She's afraid to touch it.

**DM hooks:**
- If Aldric shows her the token, she will recognize the stamp pattern — Caspar's. Her face will do the work before she says anything.
- If the player presses on the auditor, she'll tell the story but minimize what it means. She told herself it was routine.
- The ledger exists if the player earns enough trust to search the workshop. DC 15 Investigation to find the false bottom without being told.
- She is being watched. A threshold observer (not Corvin) notes any unusual visitor to her shop.
