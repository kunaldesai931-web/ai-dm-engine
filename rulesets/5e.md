# 5e Adjudication Reference

The engine owns every die; this file tells the DM *when* and *how* to ask the engine to roll, and how to read the result back into the fiction.

## Abilities & skills

Six abilities: **STR** (Strength), **DEX** (Dexterity), **CON** (Constitution), **INT** (Intelligence), **WIS** (Wisdom), **CHA** (Charisma).

Standard skills, mapped to their governing ability:

- **STR**: Athletics
- **DEX**: Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Stealth
- **INT**: Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion
- **WIS**: Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival
- **CHA**: Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion

CON has no skills (it governs Constitution saves and concentration only).

## DC ladder

| Difficulty        | DC  | Roll?            |
|-------------------|-----|------------------|
| Trivial           | —   | No roll; narrate success |
| Easy              | 10  | Roll             |
| Medium            | 15  | Roll             |
| Hard              | 20  | Roll             |
| Very hard         | 25  | Roll             |
| Nearly impossible | 30  | Roll             |

**Rule:** only call for a check when failure is *interesting* AND success is *uncertain*. If either is false — if failure has no consequence, or the outcome is not in doubt — narrate the result instead of rolling.

## Advantage / disadvantage

Grant **advantage** when circumstances clearly favor the actor: superior position, help from an ally, exploiting a target's condition (e.g. attacking a prone or restrained creature in melee), or a fitting class feature. Pass `--adv` to the engine.

Impose **disadvantage** when circumstances clearly hinder: poor visibility, awkward footing, the actor is poisoned or frightened, attacking at long range, or attacking a prone target at range. Pass `--dis` to the engine.

Advantage and disadvantage do not stack and do not accumulate: if at least one source of each applies, they cancel and the roll is straight (neither flag).

## Conditions

Core conditions: **prone, grappled, restrained, poisoned, frightened, stunned, blinded, charmed, paralyzed, unconscious**.

Do not paraphrase a condition's mechanics from memory — use `srd condition <name>` for the exact effects (e.g. `srd condition restrained`) before applying them.

## Combat

Always drive combat through the engine combat tracker. Never hand-wave HP, initiative, or turn order.

- `combat start` — roll initiative and open the encounter
- `attack` — resolve an attack roll against AC
- `damage` — apply damage to a tracked combatant
- `combat next` — advance to the next combatant's turn
- `combat status` — show current HP, conditions, and turn order

## Rests

- **Short rest**: spend Hit Dice to recover HP — `rest --type short --hitDice N` (N = Hit Dice spent). Recharges short-rest resources.
- **Long rest**: full reset — `rest --type long`. Restores HP, half of max Hit Dice, and long-rest resources.

## Reminder

The DM consults this file because `state.meta.rulesetId` resolves to `5e`. A campaign running a different system points its `rulesetId` at a different file in this directory — adjudicate from the file the state names, never from assumption.
